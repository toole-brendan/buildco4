## Key Findings

1. **Depot maintenance is the core market.** Bucket 1 (Scheduled Depot Maintenance) represents 48% of the TAM at $13.6B. Within this, OMN funds the labor and overhead ($10.9B) while OPN funds the material and equipment ($2.4B) for the same physical availabilities.

2. **Modernization is the fastest-growing segment.** Bucket 3 ($6.6B, 23% of TAM) is driven by DDG Mod ($1.0B), SEWIP ($461M), CANES ($530M+), Ship Missile Support ($456M), and fleet-wide combat system refresh. This spending grows as the fleet ages and the threat environment demands capability upgrades.

3. **The MSC transfer inflates FY26.** The $1.584B MSC M&R transfer from NWCF to OMN in FY26 is an accounting change, not new work. Excluding it, underlying Bucket 1 growth is ~5%, not 10%.

4. **RCOH dominates Bucket 4 and is inherently lumpy.** CVN 74 STENNIS has been in RCOH since 2021 ($483M cost-to-complete in FY26). CVN 75 TRUMAN starts FY26 ($1.78B). When two RCOHs overlap, Bucket 4 spikes. When none are active, it drops to ~$200M (LCAC SLEP + LCC ESLP + CG SLEPs).

5. **Public yards handle ~27% of the work by funding flow.** The four naval shipyards ($7.8B) perform nearly all submarine depot work and a share of carrier work. Surface combatant depot maintenance flows almost entirely to private industry. The private-industry addressable market is roughly **$18-22B** of the $28.6B total.

6. **Bucket 6 is a floor, not an estimate.** The $228M identified for availability support (berthing, SIOP, facilities) captures only explicitly identifiable lines. Scaffolding, crane services, shore power, drydock services, environmental compliance, and force protection are embedded within Bucket 1 availability contracts. True Bucket 6 could be $1-2B if separated.

7. **Coast Guard is 3% of the market but growing.** USCG in-service vessel work (~$848M) is small relative to Navy but includes growing SLEP/MMA programs (WMEC SLEP ramping to $76M, WMSL MMA starting FY26). The Heritage-class OPC will drive future MMA demand.

8. **84% of the TAM is high-confidence.** $23.9B comes from named budget lines with clear boundaries. The $4.4B medium-confidence portion (15%) is driven by in-service share estimates on OPN BA2/BA4 lines and the USCG O&S vessel share assumption. Only $228M (1%) is low-confidence.

## Methodology

- **Source:** FY2026 President's Budget justification books -- 9 books processed (OMN, OMN Vol2, OPN BA1/BA2/BA4/BA5-8, SCN, USCG, NWCF)
- **Taxonomy:** Six work-type buckets derived from official Navy/CG definitions (JFMM, OPNAVINST 4700.7N, NEM), budget structure (OMN/OPN/SCN appropriation logic), and contract language (FPDS PSC codes, award descriptions)
- **Architecture:** Medallion method -- Bronze (raw extraction), Silver (canonicalization, disposition, overlap resolution), Gold (bucket classification, TAM table, reconciliation)
- **Figures:** All in then-year dollars as presented in budget books, thousands ($K)
- **Performer data:** OMN Vol2 OP-5A shipyard data used for public/private split context (not additive to TAM)
