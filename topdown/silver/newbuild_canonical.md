## Disposition Decisions

### 1. L49 Completion of PY Shipbuilding Programs — double-count check

The P-1 detail table shows individual program lines (L8 Virginia, L13 DDG, etc.) with their own CTC items for FY24 and FY25. L49 ($1,690,846K in FY26) appears as a **separate line item** with its own appropriation. The MEMO NON ADD items under L49 are breakouts showing which programs the L49 money goes to.

**Key finding:** Individual program CTC items (shown under L8, L13, etc.) appear in FY24 and FY25 columns only. L49 appears in FY26 only. This is because FY26 is the "request" year — prior-year ship completion funding for FY26 is consolidated into L49 rather than shown under individual program lines. **No double-count.**

The RCOH ($483,100) and LCAC ($15,480) MEMO items under L49 are the **same dollars** already shown in the gold table as separate Bucket 5 line items. The gold table sourced these from L10/L47 Resource Summary tables deeper in the SCN book, but the P-1 L49 MEMO confirms the amounts match. **Verified: no double-count for Bucket 5 either.**

**Disposition:** L49 Bucket 1 share = $1,192,266K in FY26. Include as a single "Prior-Year Completion" line item in Bucket 1.

### 2. DDG-1000 completion ($52K FY26)

DDG-1000 hulls are all delivered but completion/CPS funding continues. This is post-delivery work funded through SCN, similar to outfitting. The 7-bucket taxonomy boundary says "PSA and everything after is in-service." However, DDG-1000 CPS work is explicitly funded through SCN (shipbuilding), not OMN/OPN, and represents shipbuilder completion work, not fleet maintenance.

**Disposition:** Keep in Bucket 1 as new construction completion. Small enough ($52K) that classification doesn't materially affect the TAM.

### 3. Auxiliary Vessels (Used Sealift) — L48

Acquisition of existing used vessels, not new construction of hulls. However, this is funded through SCN and represents the government buying ships for the fleet. The 7-bucket taxonomy includes "vessel acquisition" in Bucket 1 scope.

**Disposition:** Include in Bucket 1. Tag as "acquisition" rather than "construction" in notes.

### 4. Outfitting (L41) — allocation

L41 is a shared outfitting line for all newbuild programs. The RCOH Resource Summary (lines 6882, 47 of SCN source) shows RCOH outfitting **separately** ($19,704 FY24, $6,660 FY25, $12,200 FY26), and the gold table already captures RCOH outfitting as its own line item. LCAC SLEP outfitting is also shown separately ($728 FY24, $0 FY25, $493 FY26).

**Disposition:** L41 is 100% Bucket 1 (newbuild outfitting). RCOH/LCAC outfitting is already in Bucket 5. No overlap.

### 5. FY26 Request vs Reconciliation

Many programs show near-zero in FY26 Request with large Reconciliation amounts (DDG-51: $10.8K Request vs $5.4B Reconcil; LPD Flight II: $0 Request vs $2.1B Reconcil; LHA: $0 vs $3.9B; MLS: $0 vs $1.96B). The FY26 Reconciliation represents the administration's full budget posture including items initially unfunded in the discretionary request.

**Disposition:** Use FY26 Total (Request + Reconcil) for TAM, consistent with how other appropriations are treated. The Total represents the government's stated intent for FY26 spending. Note the Request/Reconcil split in the gold table for transparency.

### 6. USCG Boats line — newbuild vs SLEP split

The USCG "Boats" line (FY24: $6,500, FY25: $6,500, FY26: $30,900) includes both new boat procurement and RB-M SLEP. The existing gold table has RB-M SLEP estimated at FY24: $1,950, FY25: $1,950, FY26: $9,270 (Low confidence).

**Disposition:** Exclude the Boats line from Bucket 1 newbuild entirely. The newbuild portion ($4,550/$4,550/$21,630) is too small and uncertain to separately track. The RB-M SLEP portion is already in Bucket 5. If needed, can add as a single "CG Boats (new)" line later. The total CG Boats line of $30.9M in FY26 is <0.1% of Bucket 1.

## Notes for Gold Pass

1. **Scale context:** Bucket 1 at ~$46.3B (FY26) is larger than the entire in-service TAM (~$28.6B). This is expected — U.S. naval shipbuilding is the single largest defense procurement account. The combined 7-bucket TAM will be ~$75B.

2. **FY-to-FY volatility:** Bucket 1 swings dramatically (FY24: $28B → FY25: $38B → FY26: $46B) driven by ship authorization timing, SFF phasing, and the large FY26 Reconciliation. This is normal for shipbuilding — individual ship authorizations create multi-billion-dollar spikes.

3. **Reconciliation significance:** $24.5B of the $47.4B FY26 SCN total is Reconciliation (52%). This is unusual and reflects a budget strategy where many ships were placed in Reconciliation rather than the discretionary request. The gold table should note this.

4. **Performer split:** Nearly 100% of Bucket 1 flows to private industry. The only public-sector involvement is SUPSHIP oversight (already in Bucket 6) and some Navy-furnished equipment. Marking all Bucket 1 as "Private Industry."

5. **Submarine dominance:** Columbia ($10.9B) + Virginia ($11.1B) = $22B, representing 47% of FY26 Bucket 1. Submarine construction is the single largest market segment.
