## 4. BLI 9020 DESCRIPTION: WHAT BA8 SPARES FUNDS

Source lines 17378-17402.

BLI 9020 provides funding to procure spares for systems/equipment budgeted in BA 01-07. It is a follow-on account supporting End Item Hardware procurements. Sub-line item breakdown:

- **(9020-10) Initial Spares** (line 17382-17383): Spares procured by NAVSEA Program Offices and other SYSCOMs to support newly procured hardware installations. Includes INCO-I/INCO-R Spares, On-Board Repair Parts (OBRPs), Maintenance Assistance Modules (MAMs), and depot stock.

- **(9020-20) COSAL Outfitting Spares** (line 17385-17386): Spares procured by Navy Working Capital Fund (NWCF) to provide OBRPs for allowance increases in initial range and depth, maintenance and modernization efforts, and configuration changes. Materiel "bought out" by DoD supply system, enabling NWCF solvency.

- **(9020-70) Lay-In Spares** (line 17388-17391): Broken out from 9020-20 beginning FY23 for greater fidelity. Lay-In spares provide wholesale level support prior to MSD then transferred to NAVSUP WSS 90 days prior to materiel support date (MSD). Inventory Augmentation addresses "holes on shelves" caused by underfunding spares in prior years. Ensures positive shelf health at MSD and NWCF solvency.

- **(9020-90) Vendor-Direct (Replenishment) Spares** (line 17393-17395): Provides Appropriation Purchase Account (APA) material for planned equipment replacement programs. Supports depot level maintenance turnaround time and depot throughput capabilities. Programs "supported for life" not through wholesale supply system/NWCF. Examples: Presidential Helo, missiles, torpedoes.

- **Interim Spares** (line 17397): Comprised of 9020-10 and 9020-90 collectively.

- **Nuclear support** (line 17399-17400): Includes Q-COSAL range and depth repair parts, special tools, and other miscellaneous portable items necessary for operation, overhaul, and repair of reactor plant equipment for each nuclear powered hull; and Gold Disk micro/miniature 2M repair.

- **Exclusions** (line 17402): Does NOT fund Labor costs/Labor hours or Operating Space Items (OSI) material.

## 5. BLI 9020 JUSTIFICATION AND CHANGE ITEMS

### 5a. Overall Justification (lines 17425-17435)
FY26 OPN Spares funding maintains operational availability of new and existing spares and repair parts for systems fielded to operational warfighters. Enhances ability to provide onboard repair parts, interim depot spares, and INCO spares to sustain Navy mission readiness.

- FY26 reduced $2.9M for Advisory and Assistance Services (EO 14222 DOGE efficiency) (line 17428-17429)
- FY26 reduced $1.5M for civilian personnel optimization (EO 14210 DOGE workforce) (line 17431-17432)
- FY26 request: $585.9M discretionary + $297.8M mandatory (reconciliation) = $883.6M total (line 17434)

### 7b. Description (lines 17859-17900)

New program line started in FY2024 specifically to address ongoing issues with execution of VIRGINIA Class (VACL) CNO maintenance availabilities. Lack of material is significantly impacting ability of shipyards to start work on time, conduct required repairs, and complete work in timely and efficient manner.

Key details:
- Funds material for VACL CNO availabilities: Extended Dry-Dock Selected Restricted Availability (EDSRA) and Depot Maintenance Period (DMP) for all VIRGINIA Class submarines (line 17861-17862).
- Previous NAVSEA policy was to wait to order contingent materials until after Start of Avail (SA00). ~40-50% of material for VACL CNO availabilities ordered after SA00, with industrial base lead times of 6-30 months (line 17866-17867).
- 688 Class historically did not have this issue due to mature class with demand history, sufficient sustainment planning, and material on shelf (line 17868-17869).
- May 2022 PEO SSN directed critique. Root causes: funding/procurement processes do not support execution; industrial base has inadequate capacity for short timelines (line 17871-17872).
- CNA modeling determined late material impacting VACL availabilities by approximately 100 days on average (line 17873-17874).
- PEO SSN developed VACL Material Strategy. Leveraging iForecast software tool for 3-5 year material forecast (lines 17879-17881).
- Bill of Material (BOM) process developed to augment iForecast, documenting exactly what material was used from past/current availabilities (lines 17880-17882). BOM includes Corporate Component Repair Program, Advanced Equipment Repair Program, Other Depot Level Repairables (line 17882).
