## 1. NWCF Business Area Structure (Lines 32-44)

The NWCF has four business areas:
- **Supply Management**: Inventory oversight; sale of aviation/shipboard components, repairables, consumables
- **Depot Maintenance**: Worldwide maintenance, engineering, logistics — repair of aircraft, engines, components, weapons systems, manufacture of parts/assemblies
- **Transportation**: Over-ocean movement of supplies; prepositioned equipment
- **Research and Development**: Weapons systems support through development, engineering, acquisition, in-service support, repair and maintenance

Source: NWCF, Lines 32-44

**CRITICAL FINDING**: The NWCF "Depot Maintenance" business area covers ONLY:
- Fleet Readiness Centers (FRCs) — aviation depot maintenance (Lines 287-309)
- Marine Corps Depot Maintenance Activity Group (MC DMAG) — ground combat systems (Lines 311-316)

**The four public naval shipyards (NNSY, PSNSY, PHNSY, PNSY) are NOT funded through NWCF.** Ship depot maintenance at public yards is funded through OMN direct appropriations. This book contains NO public-yard workload data, NO per-shipyard breakdowns, NO public-vs-private performer splits for ship depot maintenance.

## 8. Supply Management — Virginia Class (VACL) Material Strategy (Lines 3988-4006)

"The Virginia Class (VACL) Material Strategy is an initiative to buy critical initial spare parts in support of VACL submarine Extended Dry-Dock Selected Restricted Availabilities (EDSRA) and Depot Modernization Periods (DMP). This financial investment provides for an enduring increase of material on-hand at the beginning of each VACL EDSRA and DMP and reduces Days of Maintenance Delay (DoMD) by an estimated 100-111 days, generating ~$100 million in DoMD cost avoidance per year, and returns our VACL submarines to the Fleet sooner."

Key figures:
- **NAVSUP has obligated $909 million towards the VACL material initiative** (Line 3994)
- **~$100 million in DoMD cost avoidance per year** (Lines 3992, 87)
- Reduces Days of Maintenance Delay (DoMD) by 100-111 days (Line 3991)
- Full benefits anticipated for Avails starting FY26 and out (Lines 3993-3994)
- FY 2026 Retail CA increased by $38 million from FY2025 in support of Submarine Wholeness (Shipyards) (Line 4103)

Source: NWCF, Lines 3988-3995, 4103

## SUMMARY OF FINDINGS FOR SILVER/GOLD LAYERS

### What this book DOES contain:
1. **NWCF Depot Maintenance = Aviation (FRCs) + Marine Corps Ground (MC DMAG) only** — ~$2.9B Navy FRC + ~$360M USMC DMAG in FY26
2. **Supply management data by weapon system** (SUBMARINE, CVN, CRUDES, etc.) — these are material/spares costs flowing through NAVSUP, relevant as input costs to depot availabilities
3. **VACL submarine material strategy**: $909M obligated; $38M FY26 increase for "Submarine Wholeness (Shipyards)"
4. **MSC M&R realignment**: $1.4B moving from NWCF to OMN in FY26 — this is MSC ship maintenance, NOT combatant ship depot maintenance
5. **R&D warfare center data** (NSWC, NUWC, etc.) — ~$20B revenue; these centers provide engineering/testing support to ship maintenance programs

### What this book does NOT contain:
1. **No public naval shipyard workload data** (NNSY, PSNSY, PHNSY, PNSY)
2. **No public-vs-private performer split** for ship depot maintenance
3. **No ship depot maintenance dollar figures** (those are in OMN under SND/Ship Depot Maintenance)
4. **No submarine/carrier/surface combatant depot maintenance cost breakdowns**
5. **No SIOP (Shipyard Infrastructure Optimization Program) data**
6. **No DLH breakdowns by individual shipyard**

### Implication for TAM analysis:
The public naval shipyards are funded as OMN direct appropriations, not through NWCF. To find public-yard workload and performer-split data, look in the OMN justification books (specifically the Ship Depot Maintenance / SND line items) or in the NAVSEA/TYCOM budget exhibits that are separate from NWCF.
